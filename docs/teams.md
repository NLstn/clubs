# Team Management

This guide explains how to create and manage teams within your club for better organization and collaboration.

## Understanding Teams

Teams are sub-groups within your club that help organize members by:
- Skill level or division
- Department or function
- Age group or category
- Project or activity
- Competition level

Teams provide:
- Better organization for large clubs
- Focused communication channels
- Team-specific events and activities
- Easier management of sub-groups

## Viewing Teams

### Your Teams

To view teams you're a member of:

1. **Navigate to Dashboard**: Go to your main dashboard
2. **Check "My Teams"**: See all teams you belong to
3. **View Team Details**: Click on a team to see:
   - Team name and description
   - Team members
   - Recent team activities
   - Team-specific events

### Club Teams

To view all teams in your club:

1. **Navigate to Your Club**: Select your club
2. **Click "Teams"**: View the complete list of teams
3. **Browse Teams**: See team names, member counts, and descriptions
4. **View Details**: Click on any team to see more information

## Creating Teams (Administrators)

As a club administrator, you can create teams to organize your members.

### Creating a New Team

1. **Open Admin Panel**: Navigate to your club's admin section
2. **Go to "Teams"**: Select the team management page
3. **Click "Add Team"**: Start creating a new team
4. **Fill in Team Details**:
   - **Team Name**: Clear, descriptive name (e.g., "Junior Division", "Competition Team")
   - **Description**: Purpose and details about the team
   - **Team Lead**: Optionally assign a team leader
5. **Save Team**: Click save to create the team

!!! success "Team Created"
    Your new team is ready for member assignments.

### Team Examples

Consider creating teams based on:

**Skill Level**:
- Beginner Team

- Advanced Team

**Age Groups**:
- Youth Team (Under 18)

- Senior Team (35+)

**Function**:
- Events Committee

**Activity Type**:
- Competition Team

## Managing Teams (Administrators)

### Editing Teams

To modify team details:

1. **Go to Teams List**: Navigate to the admin teams page
2. **Select Team**: Click on the team you want to edit
3. **Make Changes**: Update team information
4. **Save Changes**: Don't forget to save your modifications

### Adding Members to Teams

Assign members to a team:

1. **Open Team Details**: Navigate to the specific team
2. **Click "Add Members"**: Start the assignment process
3. **Select Members**: Choose members from your club list
4. **Assign Role**: Set member role (Team Member or Team Lead)
5. **Save**: Confirm the assignments

!!! note "Member Notification"
    Members are notified when they're added to a team.

### Removing Members from Teams

If you need to remove a member from a team:

1. **Navigate to Team**: Go to the team's member list
2. **Select Member**: Find the member to remove
3. **Click "Remove"**: Use the remove option
4. **Confirm**: Confirm the removal

### Team Leaders

Designate team leaders to help manage the team:

**Team Leader Responsibilities**:
- Organize team activities
- Communicate with team members
- Report to club administrators
- Coordinate team participation in events

**Assigning Team Leaders**:
1. Navigate to team members
2. Select a member
3. Change their role to "Team Lead"
4. Save the change

## Team Activities

### Team-Specific Events

Create events just for team members:

1. **Create Event**: Use the regular event creation process
2. **Set Team Filter**: Mark the event as team-specific
3. **Select Team**: Choose which team(s) the event is for
4. **Save Event**: Only team members can see and RSVP

### Team Communications

Keep team members informed:

1. **Team News**: Post news visible only to team members
2. **Team Notifications**: Send announcements to the entire team
3. **Team Messages**: Facilitate team communication

### Team Shifts

Assign shifts to entire teams:

1. **Create Shift**: Use the shift creation process
2. **Assign to Team**: Select the team instead of individual members
3. **All team members**: Are assigned to the shift
4. **Coordinate**: Team members work together

## Team Organization Strategies

### Small Clubs (Under 20 Members)

- May not need many teams
- Use teams for special committees or projects
- Keep organization simple

### Medium Clubs (20-50 Members)

- Create 2-4 teams based on main divisions
- Use for competition levels or age groups
- Helps distribute management responsibilities

### Large Clubs (50+ Members)

- Create multiple teams for effective organization
- Consider hierarchical structure
- Assign team leaders to help manage
- Use teams for all major activities

## Best Practices

### Creating Effective Teams

1. **Clear Purpose**: Each team should have a clear role
2. **Appropriate Size**: Keep teams at manageable sizes (5-15 members ideal)
3. **Balanced Distribution**: Distribute members fairly across teams
4. **Strong Leadership**: Assign capable team leaders
5. **Regular Review**: Periodically review team structure

### Team Communication

1. **Regular Updates**: Keep team members informed
2. **Team Meetings**: Schedule regular team gatherings
3. **Clear Channels**: Establish communication methods
4. **Inclusive**: Ensure all team members can participate
5. **Responsive**: Address team questions and concerns quickly

### Team Building

1. **Team Events**: Create team-specific activities
2. **Recognize Achievement**: Celebrate team successes
3. **Foster Collaboration**: Encourage teamwork
4. **Build Identity**: Develop team culture and identity
5. **Encourage Bonding**: Facilitate member connections

### Team Management

1. **Monitor Engagement**: Track team participation
2. **Balance Workload**: Ensure fair distribution of responsibilities
3. **Support Leaders**: Provide resources and support to team leaders
4. **Address Issues**: Handle team conflicts promptly
5. **Adapt Structure**: Adjust teams as club needs change

## Team Transitions

### Adding New Members to Teams

When new members join:
1. Assess their skill level or interests
2. Assign to appropriate team
3. Introduce them to team members
4. Provide team orientation
5. Monitor their integration

### Moving Members Between Teams

If a member needs to change teams:
1. Discuss the reason for the change
2. Remove from current team
3. Add to new team
4. Notify both teams
5. Update any team-specific assignments

### Disbanding Teams

If a team is no longer needed:
1. Notify all team members
2. Complete pending team activities
3. Archive team records
4. Reassign members if needed
5. Document the decision

## Team Hierarchy

For large clubs, consider hierarchical structure:

### Main Teams
- Top-level organizational divisions
- Examples: Competitive, Recreational, Social

### Sub-Teams
- Divisions within main teams
- Examples: Competition Team A, Competition Team B

### Special Teams
- Cross-cutting or temporary teams
- Examples: Event Committee, Fundraising Team

## Troubleshooting

### Can't Create Teams?

- Verify you have administrator privileges
- Ensure you're in the correct club
- Check that required fields are filled

### Member Not Appearing in Team?

- Refresh the page to see updates
- Verify the member was successfully added
- Check that the member is still active in the club

### Team Not Visible to Members?

- Ensure team is marked as active
- Check member permissions
- Verify team settings

### Can't Assign Team Leader?

- Ensure member has appropriate permissions
- Verify member is active in the club
- Check that you have administrator privileges

### Team Events Not Showing?

- Verify event is marked as team-specific
- Check that correct team is selected
- Ensure members are properly assigned to team

### Need Help?

Contact your club administrator or system administrator for assistance with team-related issues.
